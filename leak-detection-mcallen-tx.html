Types of Leaks We Detect

Every leak is different. Here are the most common issues McAllen homeowners face and how we solve them.

Slab Leak Detection

Water leaking beneath your concrete foundation is one of the most damaging and expensive problems a McAllen home can face. We use physical inspection, pressure checks, and 70 years of pattern recognition to narrow down the source — checking water meters, isolating lines, and identifying the telltale signs that pinpoint which section of slab is compromised.

Water Line Leaks

A leak in your main water line can waste thousands of gallons per month and spike your water bill without any visible sign. We trace the line from meter to house, check for wet spots, pressure drops, and visible damage to isolate the section and find the breach.

Hidden Pipe Leaks

Leaks inside walls, ceilings, and under floors cause mold, structural damage, and rot long before you see them. We inspect for water stains, moisture patterns, and pressure inconsistencies to catch these hidden leaks early — before a small drip becomes a major remodel.

Gas Line Leak Detection

Gas leaks are a safety emergency. If you smell rotten eggs or hear hissing near a gas connection, call us immediately. We check every joint, connection, and fitting to locate the source and make it safe. Gas line work is one of our core strengths — we are a licensed master plumber with decades of gas line experience.

Know the Signs

Warning Signs You May Have a Hidden Leak

Most leaks start small and grow silently. If you notice any of these signs in your McAllen home, do not wait — call us for an inspection.

Unexplained Water Bill Spike

If your water bill jumped 25% or more without a change in usage, there is likely a leak somewhere in your system that is running 24/7.

Warm Spots on the Floor

Hot water line leaks under your slab create warm patches on tile or concrete floors. This is a classic slab leak indicator in South Texas homes.

Mold or Musty Smell

Persistent mold or a musty odor — especially in rooms without obvious moisture — often means water is collecting inside a wall or under your foundation.

Sound of Running Water

If you hear water running when every faucet and appliance is off, water is moving somewhere it should not be. That is a leak.

Foundation Cracks

New cracks in your foundation, walls, or flooring can indicate a slab leak eroding the soil beneath your home. This is especially common in the RGV's clay-heavy soil.

Leak Detection FAQ

How do I know if I have a hidden water leak?

Common signs include unexplained spikes in your water bill, the sound of running water when all fixtures are off, warm or damp spots on your floor, mold or mildew smell, and cracks in your foundation. If you notice any of these, call Valley Modern Plumbing at (956) 686-3687 for a hands-on leak inspection.

How much does leak detection cost in McAllen, TX?

Leak detection costs vary depending on the type and location of the leak. A standard inspection typically ranges from $150 to $400. Slab leak detection may cost more due to specialized equipment. Contact us for a specific quote — we always provide transparent pricing before starting any work.

Can you detect leaks under a concrete slab?

We inspect for slab leaks using physical methods — checking water meters for movement when all fixtures are off, isolating hot and cold lines, looking for warm spots on floors, and examining pressure drops. For leaks that require specialized electronic detection equipment, we will be upfront about it and advise you on next steps.

How long does a leak detection inspection take?

Most leak detection inspections take 1 to 2 hours depending on the complexity. We provide a full report of our findings and recommend the most cost-effective repair approach.

Do you repair leaks after finding them?

Absolutely. Valley Modern Plumbing handles both the detection and the repair. Once we locate the leak, we discuss repair options with you and can often complete the fix the same day.
