Types of Leaks We Detect

Every leak is different. Here are the most common issues McAllen homeowners face and how we solve them.

Slab Leak Detection

Water leaking beneath your concrete foundation is one of the most damaging and expensive problems a McAllen home can face. We use pressure testing and acoustic listening equipment to locate the exact point of failure — no unnecessary jackhammering.

Water Line Leaks

A leak in your main water line can waste thousands of gallons per month and spike your water bill without any visible sign. We trace the line from meter to house, isolate the section, and pinpoint the breach.

Hidden Pipe Leaks

Leaks inside walls, ceilings, and under floors cause mold, structural damage, and rot long before you see them. We detect these hidden leaks early — before a small drip becomes a major remodel.

Gas Line Leak Detection

Gas leaks are a safety emergency. If you smell rotten eggs or hear hissing near a gas connection, call us immediately. We perform pressure tests and electronic detection to locate the source and make it safe.

Know the Signs

Warning Signs You May Have a Hidden Leak

Most leaks start small and grow silently. If you notice any of these signs in your McAllen home, do not wait — call us for an inspection.

Unexplained Water Bill Spike

If your water bill jumped 25% or more without a change in usage, there is likely a leak somewhere in your system that is running 24/7.

Warm Spots on the Floor

Hot water line leaks under your slab create warm patches on tile or concrete floors. This is a classic slab leak indicator in South Texas homes.

Mold or Musty Smell

Persistent mold or a musty odor — especially in rooms without obvious moisture — often means water is collecting inside a wall or under your foundation.

Sound of Running Water

If you hear water running when every faucet and appliance is off, water is moving somewhere it should not be. That is a leak.

Foundation Cracks

New cracks in your foundation, walls, or flooring can indicate a slab leak eroding the soil beneath your home. This is especially common in the RGV's clay-heavy soil.
