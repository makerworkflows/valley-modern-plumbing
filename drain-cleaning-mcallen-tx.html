Our Services

Drain Cleaning Services We Provide

From a single clogged sink to a full sewer line backup, we handle every drain problem McAllen homeowners and businesses face.

Kitchen Drain Cleaning

Grease, food particles, and soap buildup are the top causes of kitchen drain clogs. We use mechanical snaking and high-pressure water to clear the line completely — not just punch a hole through the grease.

Bathroom Drain Cleaning

Hair, soap scum, and toothpaste create stubborn clogs in bathroom sinks, showers, and tubs. We clear the full drain line — not just the trap — so the problem does not come back in two weeks.

Main Sewer Line Cleaning

When multiple drains back up at once, the problem is in your main sewer line. Tree roots, sediment buildup, and pipe collapse are common in older McAllen neighborhoods. We use heavy-duty cable machines and camera inspections to diagnose and clear the blockage.

Floor Drain & Utility Drains

Garage floor drains, laundry room drains, and utility drains collect debris that standard cleaning misses. We clear these lines and check for proper slope and drainage to prevent future standing water.

Commercial Drain Service

Restaurants, retail spaces, and commercial kitchens need drain cleaning that meets health code standards. We handle grease trap lines, floor drains, and high-volume waste lines for McAllen businesses.

Know the Signs

When to Call a Professional for Drain Cleaning

DIY solutions work for minor clogs. But these signs mean it is time to call a licensed plumber before the problem gets expensive.

Multiple Slow Drains

If more than one drain in your home is running slow, the clog is likely in your main line — not individual fixtures. This requires professional equipment to clear.

Recurring Clogs

A clog that keeps coming back after you clear it means the real blockage is deeper in the line. Chemical cleaners and plungers only reach the surface — the root cause needs professional attention.

Sewage Smell

A sewage odor coming from your drains means waste is not flowing properly. This is a health concern and should be addressed immediately — especially in the RGV heat.

Water Backing Up

Water coming up through a floor drain, shower, or toilet when you run the washing machine is a clear sign of a main line blockage. Do not wait — this gets worse fast.

Gurgling Sounds

Gurgling from your toilet or drains when water runs elsewhere in the house means air is trapped in the line by a partial blockage. The line needs to be cleaned before it becomes a full backup.

Common Questions

Drain Cleaning FAQ

How much does drain cleaning cost in McAllen, TX?

Most standard drain cleaning services range from $100 to $350 depending on the severity and location of the clog. Main sewer line cleaning may cost more. We always provide upfront pricing before starting work — no surprises.

What causes drains to clog?

The most common causes are grease buildup in kitchen drains, hair and soap scum in bathroom drains, tree root intrusion in sewer lines, and mineral scale from McAllen's hard water. Older homes with cast iron or galvanized pipes are especially prone to buildup.

Should I use chemical drain cleaners?

We strongly recommend against them. They provide temporary relief but corrode your pipes over time — especially older pipes common in McAllen homes built before the 1980s. Professional mechanical cleaning is safer, more effective, and actually solves the problem.

How often should drains be professionally cleaned?

For most McAllen homes, professional drain cleaning every 1 to 2 years prevents major clogs. Kitchens with heavy use and homes with older plumbing benefit from annual cleaning. If you notice slow drains more than once, it is time to call.

Can you clean the main sewer line?

Yes. Main sewer line cleaning is one of our core services. We use professional-grade cable machines and hydro-jetting equipment to clear blockages, including tree root intrusion. We can also perform camera inspections to identify the exact cause and condition of your sewer line.
